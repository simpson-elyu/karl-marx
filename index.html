Explanation

Marx's theory posits that a society's economic Base (the means and relations of production) determines its Superstructure (politics, law, culture, and ideology).

  • The Base (Foundation):
    • (D) Factories & Tools and (E) Land & Resources are the Means of Production.
    • (F) Class Relations are the Relations of Production, defining who owns the means and who works them.
  • The Superstructure (Built on top):
    • (A) The State & Law and (C) Art & Culture are institutions that reflect and protect the economic base.
    • (B) Dominant Ideology is the capstone, making the unequal relations seem natural and inevitable.
  • The Engine of Change:
    • (G) Class Struggle is the inherent contradiction within capitalism. The conflict between the owners (Bourgeoisie) and workers (Proletariat) intensifies over time.
    • (H) This struggle ultimately leads to a Revolution, which overthrows the old system and establishes a new economic base, leading to a new historical epoch.
