Explanation

Marx's theory posits that a society's economic Base (the means and relations of production) determines its Superstructure (politics, law, culture, and ideology).

  • The Base (Foundation): (D) Factories & Tools, (E) Land & Resources (Means of Production); (F) Class Relations (Relations of Production).
  • The Superstructure: (A) The State & Law, (C) Art & Culture, and (B) Dominant Ideology all serve to legitimize the Base.
  • The Engine of Change: (G) Class Struggle is the inherent contradiction that leads to (H) Revolution, creating a new historical epoch.
